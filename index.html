KleanAIR

Bringing government and citizens together for a better climate

Our project is Kleaner AIR. It is a data-driven decision-making application that leverages AI and machine learning to help policy makers and the general public mitigate the impact of climate change and improve air quality. We intend to collaborate with local and international partners to harness IoT data so that we can predict air quality hazards, such as pollution, pollen, and wildfires, and present this information in easy-to-understand visualisations. In the future we wish to make open source IoT-sensors for areas which are not monitored already.

As a politician who wishes to re-establish trust in government, you should take a look in our direction. By using the app, you can make commitments to changes in Nairobi (planting vegetation or making infrastructure changes), display it to the citizens, and build trust.

As a citizen you can follow the changes while monitoring the air quality in the part of the city where you live. This project still has to be realised. We are in the progress of researching for our first prototype and establishing new partnerships.

WHAT WE DO

We wish to create a lasting impact and a fundamental change in Nairobi using Smart City technologies with IoT data and AI. We wish to improve health, create transparency in government, and create better cities to live in.

Problem

Like anywhere else in the world, climate change is affecting Kenya. When it comes to greenhouse gas emissions, Kenya has improvements but these are based on fuel export and cement production. Mitigation of climate change, also in cities, are vital for the citizens’ lives and livelihood.



Overall Kenyans have a low trust in their politicians. Up to 66% distrusts the government per default, according to the Edelman Trust Barometer 2022.



Our mission

We wish to create a lasting impact and a fundamental change in Nairobi and potentially other cities using Smart City technologies. We wish to improve health, create transparency in government, and create better cities to live in.



This project seeks to address all three issues: Air pollution, climate change, and distrust in politicians. We will use IoT-data and AI to combat these issues while greening Nairobi and making it more livable for its citizens.
